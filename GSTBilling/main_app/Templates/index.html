Features of GST: Top 10 Features of Goods and Services Tax in India

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a nationwide indirect tax levied on businesses with the goal of making India a unified market. It is a single tax applied on the product life cycle of goods and services. There are three types of GST — central GST (CGST), state GST (SGST), and integrated GST (IGST).

If You Want To Know More About GST Check Government Official Site

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) is an indirect federal sales tax. It is a comprehensive, multistage, destination-based tax that has subsumed almost all the indirect taxes except a few state taxes. GST is a tax that we need to pay on supply of goods & services. It is based on the “Destination principle” and is applied on goods and services at the place where final/actual consumption happens. GST is expected to replace a myriad of indirect taxes such as VAT, customs duty, Excise, CST, Service Tax, Entertainment Tax with a single tax called the Goods and Services Tax
